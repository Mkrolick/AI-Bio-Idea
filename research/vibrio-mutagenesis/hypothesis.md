# Hypothesis: Vibrio natriegens Continuous Mutagenesis Platform

## Core Idea
Combine *Vibrio natriegens* (9-minute doubling time, ~7x faster than E. coli) with an engineered in vivo continuous mutagenesis system to perform deep mutational scanning in hours instead of weeks.

## Why This Matters
Deep mutational scanning (DMS) is critical for understanding protein function, drug resistance, and enzyme engineering. Current DMS workflows take weeks: build a library, transform, grow, select, sequence. V. natriegens' extreme growth rate could compress this dramatically.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can existing mutagenesis systems (EvolvR, MutaT7, PACE) be ported to V. natriegens?
- What is the mutation rate achievable per generation in V. natriegens?
- How do you couple mutagenesis with high-throughput readout in this organism?
- Does the fast doubling time introduce unique challenges (repair overwhelm, fitness effects)?
