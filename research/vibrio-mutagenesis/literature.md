# Literature Review - Vibrio natriegens Mutagenesis

## V. natriegens Biology & Growth
- **Weinstock et al. (2016).** *Nature Methods* 13:849-851. Seminal paper: <10 min doubling, initial genetic tools.
- **Hoffart et al. (2017).** *Appl. Environ. Microbiol.* 83:e01614-17. Doubling time 9.4 min in BHIN. 9-13x higher volumetric productivity than E. coli.
- **Schulz et al. (2018).** *Metabolic Engineering* 45:119-129. ~24 min doubling in minimal media.

## Genetic Tools (Mature)
- **Dalia et al. (2017).** *ACS Synth. Biol.* 6:1650-1655. **MuGENT**: multiplex genome editing via natural transformation. 100-fold PHB increase targeting 9 genes in 1 week.
- **Wei et al. (2024).** *PNAS Nexus* 3:pgad444. Zero-capital natural plasmid transformation. ~10^6 cfu/ug, 50 min hands-on.
- **Stukenberg et al. (2022).** *Comm. Biol.* 5:265. **NT-CRISPR**: markerless, scarless editing up to 100% efficiency.
- **Stukenberg et al. (2021).** *ACS Synth. Biol.* 10:2236-2247. **Marburg Collection**: 191 standardized Golden Gate parts.
- **Thuronyi lab (2025).** *NAR* 53:gkaf580. **Vnat Collection**: 220+ parts, 300-fold improved assembly.
- **Tschirhart et al. (2019).** *ACS Synth. Biol.* 8:2069-2079. T7-based expression, promoters, RBS, reporters.
- **Corts et al. (2019).** *Nature Microbiology* 4:1105-1113. CRISPRi functional genomics.

## Directed Evolution IN V. natriegens (Already Published)
- **Medin et al. (2023).** *ACS Synth. Biol.* 12:3680-3694. **MP6 mutagenesis in V. natriegens** — first non-E. coli use. 2200-fold mutation rate increase. 3 rounds of mutagenesis + selection for REE binding.

## CRITICAL: VinORep (Feb 2026 Preprint)
- **Tian, Rehm, Chin et al. (2026).** bioRxiv 2026.02.20.706958v1. **"A portable orthogonal replication system enables continuous gene evolution near the biological speed limit."**
- Orthogonal DNA replication in V. natriegens. ~10^-4 substitutions/base/generation. 77 kb replicon.
- **Evolved new function in 16 hours** by combining high mutation rate + fast doubling time.
- From Jason Chin lab (MRC-LMB Cambridge). THIS DIRECTLY PREEMPTS OUR HYPOTHESIS.

## Other Continuous Evolution Systems
- **PACE (Esvelt et al., 2011).** *Nature* 472:499-503. E. coli only (M13 phage). Not portable to V. natriegens.
- **OrthoRep:** Yeast only (S. cerevisiae).
- **BacORep (Tian et al., 2023).** *Nature Chem. Biol.* 19:1504-1512. B. thuringiensis.
- **EcORep (2024).** *Science.* E. coli. Precursor to VinORep.
- **ITMU (Gu et al., 2025).** *Trends Biotech.* Broad-host-range (E. coli, P. putida, C. glutamicum, Y. lipolytica). RSF1010 replicon. Not yet in V. natriegens but could work.
- **MutaT7:** T7 RNAP + deaminase. Works in E. coli, yeast, plants, human cells, B. subtilis.

## Companies
- **Telesis Bio (Vmax X2):** Commercial V. natriegens strains since 2017.
- **New Breed Biosciences (NBx CyClone):** Feb 2026 preprint "Outpacing E. coli" — engineered V. natriegens cloning host.

## ORIGINALITY ASSESSMENT: LOW
VinORep (Feb 2026) from the Chin lab directly does continuous evolution in V. natriegens with an orthogonal replication system. Our hypothesis is essentially preempted. The only remaining angle would be a DIFFERENT mutagenesis approach (e.g., porting PACE to V. natriegens via engineered phage, or combining MuGENT with high-throughput selection) but the core idea of "fast organism + continuous mutagenesis" is now published.
