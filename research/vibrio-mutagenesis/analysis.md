# Technical Feasibility Analysis - Vibrio natriegens Mutagenesis

## Approach Details
The core idea — combining V. natriegens' fast doubling time with continuous mutagenesis — has been directly implemented by the Chin lab's VinORep system (Feb 2026 preprint).

## Feasibility Assessment
Technically feasible — the Chin lab proved it works. But this means the idea is no longer original.

## Possible Pivot Angles
1. **PACE in V. natriegens** — requires engineering a phage system that infects V. natriegens (M13 doesn't). Novel but very hard.
2. **MuGENT + high-throughput selection** — use natural competence for massively parallel oligo-mediated mutagenesis combined with automated droplet-based selection.
3. **Growth-coupled continuous evolution** — link target protein function to V. natriegens growth rate for autonomous selection.

## Risk Analysis
Main risk: the Chin lab preprint will likely be published in a high-profile journal, making the "V. natriegens + continuous evolution" concept widely known before the competition deadline.

## Originality Score: 3/10 (core hypothesis preempted by VinORep)
## Tractability Score: 8/10 (proven feasible)

## RECOMMENDATION: PRUNE or PIVOT
