# Hypothesis: Cell-Free Genetic Circuit Prototyping with Linear DNA

## Core Idea
Use cell-free TX-TL systems with linear DNA (PCR products, not plasmids) to prototype genetic circuits and test gene expression in hours instead of days. Skip cloning, skip transformation, skip overnight growth — go directly from PCR product to functional protein output in 2-4 hours.

## Why This Matters
The design-build-test cycle in synthetic biology is bottlenecked by cloning and transformation (3-5 days per cycle). Cell-free systems with linear DNA eliminate these steps entirely. A genetic part can be designed, synthesized as primers, PCR-amplified, and tested in cell-free in a single afternoon.

## Key Technical Questions
- How reliable is gene expression from linear DNA in cell-free vs. plasmid?
- Can you prototype multi-gene circuits (not just single proteins)?
- What is the quantitative correlation between cell-free and in vivo behavior?
- How do you protect linear DNA from nuclease degradation in cell-free extracts?
- Is there a specific innovation here, or is this already standard practice?
