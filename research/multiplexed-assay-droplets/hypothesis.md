# Hypothesis: Massively Parallel Biochemical Assays via Encoded Droplet Microreactors

## Core Idea
Use optically barcoded picoliter droplets as individual microreactors to run thousands of biochemical assays simultaneously in a single tube. Each droplet contains a unique combination of reagents (encoded by fluorescent barcodes), enabling massively parallel screening of conditions, concentrations, or variants in minutes instead of weeks of plate-based assays.

## Why This Matters
Many experiments (enzyme kinetics, drug dose-response, buffer optimization, substrate screening) are done serially in multi-well plates, one condition at a time. This is slow and reagent-intensive. Droplet microfluidics can compress thousands of conditions into a single experiment, but current systems are complex. A simplified, accessible droplet platform could democratize massively parallel experimentation.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can droplet barcoding be made simple and reliable enough for routine use?
- How do you read out results from thousands of individual droplets?
- What assay types are compatible with picoliter volumes?
- How does this compare to existing droplet platforms (10x Genomics, RainDance)?
- Is there a novel angle on the encoding/readout that makes this more accessible?
