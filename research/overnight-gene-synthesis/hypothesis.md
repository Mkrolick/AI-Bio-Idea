# Hypothesis: Desktop Enzymatic DNA Synthesizer for Overnight Gene Construction

## Core Idea
Design a benchtop enzymatic DNA synthesis device that can produce sequence-verified, cloning-ready gene constructs overnight (< 12 hours), eliminating the 1-2 week wait for commercial gene synthesis orders. Uses template-independent enzymatic synthesis (TdT-based) combined with real-time nanopore quality monitoring.

## Why This Matters
Gene synthesis ordering is a hidden bottleneck — researchers often wait 1-2 weeks for constructs, and iteration cycles (design-build-test) are gated by this turnaround. Companies like DNA Script and Ansa Biotechnologies are working on enzymatic synthesis, but a specific architecture combining real-time QC with rapid assembly could compress the timeline further.

## Key Technical Questions
- What is the current state of enzymatic DNA synthesis fidelity and length limits?
- Can real-time nanopore sequencing be integrated as inline QC during synthesis?
- What assembly method works best for joining enzymatically synthesized oligos into genes?
- How does this compare to what DNA Script/Ansa are already building?
- Is there a novel angle here or is this already being done?
