# Hypothesis: Template-Free Protein Printer

## Core Idea
Build a system that directly synthesizes proteins from amino acid feedstocks, bypassing DNA entirely. Instead of gene synthesis -> cloning -> expression -> purification (weeks), directly fabricate the polypeptide chain (hours).

## Why This Matters
The DNA-to-protein pipeline is the biggest time sink in structural biology, biochemistry, and drug discovery. Every protein study starts with weeks of cloning and expression optimization. A "protein printer" that skips DNA entirely would be transformative.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS) be extended to full-length proteins reliably?
- What about native chemical ligation for assembling fragments?
- Could a ribosome-inspired machine be engineered for template-free synthesis?
- How do you handle folding for directly synthesized proteins?
- What are the length/complexity limits?
