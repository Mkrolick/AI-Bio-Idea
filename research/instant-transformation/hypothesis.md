# Hypothesis: Electroporation-Free Instant Bacterial Transformation

## Core Idea
Replace chemical/electroporation transformation (which requires competent cell prep, recovery periods, and plating) with a continuous-flow membrane permeabilization system that transforms bacteria in minutes with >10x higher efficiency.

## Why This Matters
Transformation is a bottleneck in every cloning, library construction, and protein expression workflow. Preparing competent cells takes hours to days. The transformation step itself requires recovery time (1 hour) and overnight plating. A near-instant, high-efficiency transformation method would cascade time savings across all downstream workflows.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can mechanical or acoustic membrane permeabilization achieve transformation-level DNA uptake?
- What about cell-penetrating peptide conjugated DNA?
- Could microfluidic squeeze-based approaches (like SQZ Biotech's platform) work for bacteria?
- How do you maintain cell viability while maximizing DNA uptake?
