# Hypothesis: 15-Minute Microfluidic Western Blot

## Core Idea
Replace the 6-8 hour traditional western blot workflow (gel casting, electrophoresis, transfer, blocking, primary antibody, secondary antibody, imaging) with a microfluidic capillary system that performs separation, capture, and detection in a single continuous-flow step in under 15 minutes.

## Why This Matters
Western blotting is one of the most common protein analysis techniques, performed millions of times per year worldwide. The current workflow is absurdly manual, takes most of a day, and has high failure rates. While companies like ProteinSimple (now Bio-Techne) offer capillary westerns, there may be room for a fundamentally faster approach using novel separation/detection chemistry.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can protein separation be achieved in microfluidic channels fast enough?
- What immobilization/capture strategy replaces membrane transfer?
- Can antibody incubation times be reduced to seconds with flow-through formats?
- How does sensitivity compare to traditional western blots?
- What already exists (Simple Western, Jess, etc.) and what's the novel angle?
