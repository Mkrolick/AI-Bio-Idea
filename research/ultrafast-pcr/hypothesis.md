# Hypothesis: Sub-Minute PCR via Photonic Thermocycling

## Core Idea
Replace conventional Peltier-based thermocycling with photonic heating (IR laser or LED arrays) of nanoliter droplets to achieve full PCR amplification in under 5 minutes, down from 1-2 hours.

## Why This Matters
PCR is the single most common molecular biology technique. Every lab runs dozens of PCRs daily. The bottleneck is thermocycling speed — heating and cooling bulk liquid is slow. By reducing reaction volumes to nanoliters and using photonic heating, thermal mass drops dramatically and cycle times collapse.

## Key Technical Questions
- What is the minimum volume for reliable PCR amplification?
- Can IR/LED heating achieve the ramp rates needed for sub-second transitions?
- How do you handle evaporation at nanoliter volumes?
- Does polymerase activity keep up at these speeds (enzyme kinetics limit)?
- What readout method works at nanoliter scale?
