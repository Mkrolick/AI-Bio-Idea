# Hypothesis: Acoustic-Driven Parallel Protein Purification

## Core Idea
Use acoustic levitation and sorting in microfluidic channels to perform multi-step protein purification (lysis, capture, wash, elution) in a continuous flow format, reducing purification from a full day to under 30 minutes.

## Why This Matters
Protein purification is a multi-hour to multi-day process that follows every expression experiment. Column chromatography is sequential, manual, and slow. Acoustic manipulation could enable massively parallel, continuous-flow purification without columns.

## Key Technical Questions
- Can acoustic forces selectively separate tagged proteins from lysate?
- What affinity capture mechanisms work in a flow-through acoustic format?
- Throughput: can you purify enough protein for downstream assays?
- How does purity compare to column chromatography?
